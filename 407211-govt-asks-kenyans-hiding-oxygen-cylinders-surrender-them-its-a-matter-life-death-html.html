Gov't Asks Kenyans Hiding Oxygen Cylinders to Surrender Them: &quot;It's a Matter of Life and Death&quot;

Publish date: 2024-07-26

- Heath Cabinet Secretary Miutahi Kagwe said Kenyans had held gas cylinders leading to a shortage in hospitals

- The CS warned that if the shortage would continue then the country was going to face an oxygen crisis at such a time that there is a high demand

- Local manufactures could not be banked on due to their plants being in disrepair

Health CS Mutahi Kagwe has appealed to Kenyans holding and hoarding oxygen cylinders to return them so that they can be refilled and released to hospitals.

Due to the cylinders' shortage, the CS said the country could be facing an imminent oxygen crisis in hospitals as the COVID-19's third wave continues to ravage.

Addressing the press on Monday, March 29, Kagwe said it was hard and expensive to procure new cylinders given how the market has been 'squeezed' internationally.

"We are appealing to anyone who is holding oxygen gas cylinders to immediately return them so that companies can use them to supply oxygen to hospitals. These cylinders are expensive and the international market is currently squeezed," he said.

CS Kagwe added it was unlawful for one to hold the cylinders while the government had asked that they be returned back.

"Supply is a problem. Each cylinder costs about Ksh.40,000. To replace them would require a lot of money. It is an offence to keep the cylinders when we have asked for them under the Public Health Act,” said Kagwe.

With the current demand for Oxygen, Kagwe said the government could not bank on the local manufactures due to their factories being faulty and in disrepair. The CS said the factories needed urgent servicing before they could resume operations.

“We have around 70 oxygen plants in Kenya. Most of these plants are in disrepair and we need to get them working very urgently. We are going to standardise the kind of oxygen plants that we have in the country because as we speak we can’t get spare parts for some of the plants that were built years essentially because they came from sources that are suspect,"

The CS stated that unless the local infrastructures necessary for Oxygen production are renovated to meet the over par standards, the government was not going to rely on the local plants.

“We need to increase the infrastructure for piping in hospitals. What is now happening is that individual cylinders are being used per patient such that we have a cylinder lying next to a patient. This is inefficient and dangerous," he added."Therefore we are asking facilties in the country to expedite the piping of oxygen. And then course these are pressurised cylinders and so anything can happen when we have something like that placed next to a patient,” said Kagwe.

This follows concerns raised by Health CAS Rashid Aman who claimed some facilities were hoarding gas cylinders thus hindering refilling and redistribution.

The health ministry has been asking the health facilities in the country to ensure that they put to good use the available cylinders for oxygen storage and distribution.
