TSC TPAD downloads: forms and manuals for teachers

Publish date: 0001-01-01

Teachers are tasked with shaping the next generation of leaders, movers, shakers, and members of society. As a result, they have a huge bearing on how successful future generations will be. With such a vital responsibility, it is important to ensure that teaching professionals are up to the task, which is where performance appraisals come in. In Kenya, performance assessment of teachers is carried out by their employer, the Teacher Service Commission (TSC), and one of the appraisal tools it is currently using are the TSC TPAD forms and manuals.

TPAD stands for Teacher Performance Appraisal and Development, and it was a system that was developed by TSC to help teachers identify areas that can be improved to enhance student learning. The system also helps to ensure that teachers are performing at their best capabilities, thus guaranteeing high teaching standards in learning institutions in the country. The TSC TPAD system is used in primary, secondary, and tertiary institutions in the country. Below, we have compiled all the information about the system, from the list of all forms, what each does, and how to download and fill them.

TSC TPAD 2019 PDF forms and manuals

Below is a list of all the TSC TPAD forms used by the Teacher Service Commission to assess the performance of teachers.

PC for heads of primary schools

This is the TSC TPAD tool that is used to assess the performance of primary school heads by the TSC. It details the responsibilities of the head teacher, and for performance assessment, features various criteria such as financial stewardship, service delivery, core mandate, procurement, cross-cutting strategies, and others.

PC for principal of secondary schools

This is the performance assessment form used to evaluate heads of secondary schools (principals). It is similar to the one used for heads of primary schools and features similar assessment criteria such as financial stewardship and discipline, service delivery, execution of core mandate, procurement processes, and cross-cutting techniques.

PC M and E termly progress report tool

This is the monitoring and evaluation (M&E) TPAD tool that is used to report the performance of an institution on a termly basis. It features section for performance target, the activities undertook, the achievement reach, deviation (from the target), reasons for deviation, and suggestions for improvement.

PC M and E monthly progress report tool

This is another monitoring and evaluation tool used to report the progress of primary and secondary institutions in the country. It is similar to the termly M&E one, with the only difference being that it has a monthly reporting period.

PC M and E annual progress report tool

This is another M&E form used to evaluate the performance of learning institutions in Kenya by the TSC. It is similar to the monthly and termly M&E TPAD appraisals above but has a reporting period of one year.

TPAD check list for heads of institution

This is the TSC TPAD appraisal used to assess the heads of institutions' maintenance of teaching standards. It contains a checklist of various items used in the teaching process, for example, master timetable, approved work schemes, lesson attendance analysis, extra-curricular activities records, performance appraisals for staff, etc.

TPAD check list for teacher professional documents

This is another performance assessment form used to assess heads of institutions. It contains a checklist for professional document that should be maintained by the head, for example, master timetable, analysed examination assessment, copies of approved work schemes, analysed lesson attendance, disciplinary and guidance counselling records, staff files, and others.

TPAD post-secondary institutions

This is a TSC TPAD used to assess the performance of lecturers, trainers, and tutors employed by the TSC in post-secondary institutions. The performance is assessed based on teaching standards criteria such as professional knowledge and ability to apply it, time management, creativity and innovativeness, lecture conduct and learner protection, safety, and discipline, professional development, promotion of extra-curricular, and collaboration with guardians, sponsors, and stakeholders.

TPAD tool for teachers

This is the TSC TPAD for teachers, used to assess their teaching performance. It is similar to the TPAD form for lecturers, with the same performance criteria.

TPAD lesson observation FormLOF

This is another TPAD for teachers’ appraisal tool that is used to assess their performance based on their teaching conduct during a lesson. It looks at various teaching criteria, which include lesson introduction and organisation, delivery of content, teaching techniques used, learner engagement/involvement, and classroom management.

TPAD lesson recovery ScheduleLRS

This is another TPAD assessment form used by TSC on teachers. It assesses a teacher's ability to recover missed lessons. The form lists the number of lessons missed, those recovered and those not recovered.

TPAD school calendar of activities

This is a TPAD form that contains an institution’s calendar of TPAD activities. It lists the various activities that must be completed, the person(s) to complete them, and the time frame by which they must be completed.

TPAD teacher lesson attendance register TLAR

It is an assessment form used to evaluate teachers based on their lesson attendance. The form is designed to be completed by class monitors and then submitted to the class teacher (lower primary classes) or deputy head teacher/deputy principal for further completion, before being finally submitted to the head teacher/principal.

TPAD tool SNE

This is a TPAD assessment used to evaluate the teaching performance of teachers involved in special needs education. It is similar the TPAD appraisals used for teachers and lecturers. It assesses teaching criteria such as professional knowledge and application, management of time, teaching innovation and creativity, learner protection and teacher conduct, extra-curricular activities promotion, professional development, and partnership with parents/guardians.

TPAD tool for heads of institutions

The form assesses the teaching performance of heads of institutions. It is similar to the TPAD assessment tools for lecturers/trainers/tutors, teachers, and teachers offering special needs education.

All these TPAD forms are available online on the TSC website. You can find out more information about them and download them by going to the website’s TPAD download section.

How to fill TSC TPAD online

TSC TPAD tools be filled online through the www.tsc.go.ke TPAD portal.

To fill the form:

  • Visit the portal and log in using your username and password. For first time users, the TSC number is both the default username and password. In case of forgotten username, you can initiate TSC TPAD password recovery by clicking here.
  • Once you are logged into the account, go to the dashboard and click on “My Institution”.
  • Click on “Create New” to start filling new appraisals
