Babu Owino to pay school fees for KCPE candidate who threatened to end her life

Publish date: 2024-03-08

- The pupil was troubled about her future after figuring her parents could not raise her fees

- The female pupil who had performed well in her 2019 KCPE wanted to kill herself out of hopelessness

- The girl's plight, however, caught the attention of Embakasi East MP Babu Owino who has promised to support her

- The legislator noted it is the community's responsibility to help the needy

Embakasi East Member of Parliament Babu Owino has promised to take up education responsibilities of a KCSE candidate who had threatened to end to her life over lack of school fees.

Babu announced he would take the young girl through her secondary education after it was reported she became depressed after realising her parents could not raise the required fees.

The MP made the announcement on Saturday, December 28, noting that it is the responsibility of the community to help the needy.

"I will pay school fees for this leaner to ensure she achieves her dreams," he tweeted.

According to Babu, he was doing so as a way of giving back to the community while relating it to his own case where he was also helped through fundraisers to become the person he is today.

"A child belongs to the community and I would like to give an opportunity to others the same way I was given through fundraising and through selling Chang’aa," he said.

According to a report by Citizen TV, the 13-year-old girl who scored 391 marks in the recently released KCPE results had allegedly wrote a note to leave behind.

The former pupil at Xaverian Primary School in Kisumu Central had received an admission letter from Asumbi Girls and was expected to join in early January 2020.

“I know it is not the only way to solve this problem, but it is the best option for me to take,” read the note.

The family then raised the issue with her former teachers so they could talk to her as they continued looking for alternative ways to raise the school fees.

This was not the first time the legislature has shown the generous side of him by offering to pay school fees for bright students who come from humble backgrounds.

In October 2019, the outspoken MP vowed to fully sponsor the education of a university student who was forced to drop out of school due to lack of fees.

Cosmas Mutua reportedly left his studies at Multimedia University and went to work as a security guard in Mombasa.

Mutua first hit the headlines when Babu posted about his plight asking anybody with information to his whereabouts to alert him.
