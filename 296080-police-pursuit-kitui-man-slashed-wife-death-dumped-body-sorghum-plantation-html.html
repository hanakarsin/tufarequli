Police in pursuit of Kitui man who slashed wife to death, dumped body in sorghum plantation

Publish date: 2024-03-31

- Authorities found the suspected murder weapon next to where the body had been dumped

- The body was discovered by a neighbour after one of her children pointed them to the direction

- Some of the neighbours claimed the suspect murdered his wife in cold blood as their two children watched

Police in Kitui County have launched a manhunt for a man alleged to have brutally murdered his 24-year-old wife in Mutomo sub-county.

The 33-year-old, identified as Muli Mulema, allegedly slit his wife's throat, beat her to death and then dumped the body in a sorghum plantation near their home in Ngaani village.

TUKO.co.ke on Tuesday, January 15, gathered that a bloody machete believed to have been used to commit the heinous crime was found at the scene.

Confirming the incident, Kitui Police Commander Anthony Kamitu said the suspect took off after dumping the body in a nearby sorghum plantation with the weapon besides the body.

"The panga that was used to chop off the head was recovered near the deceased body. It had fresh blood, meaning she was killed early this morning (on Monday)," Kamitu said.

The deceased left behind two children aged two years and 10 months old.

Her body was moved to Mutomo Hospital mortuary awaiting postmortem.

Neighbours said they were attracted by the cry of the deceased mother's two-year-old boy who led them to where the mother's body had been dumped.

According to some of the neighbours, the suspect on the run killed his wife as their children watched.

Story by Trizah Mwikali, Kitui County
