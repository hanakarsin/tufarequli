Yul Edochie Speaks After Ailing Gospel Singer Publicly Begs For Funds

Publish date: 2024-08-05

Nollywood actor, Yul Edochie has called on top celebrities to support the popular gospel singer, Chinedu Nwadike who has  been diagnosed with chronic Kidney disease.

Naija News reports that the  Imo State indigene cried out for help after he was admitted to the Zenith Medical Centre Abuja.

The Nollywood actor via his Instagram page pleaded with their colleagues to help raise the funds the popular singer needed for his treatment.

The ailing singer in the video shared on social media revealed that funds provided to him by socialite, E-Money is what has been sustaining him since he got to Abuja.

He said: “I am calling for help from Nigerians. All the Igbo sons and daughters, Nigerians at large. I am right here now at Zenith Medical Center, Abuja. Urgent money is needed.

“I am calling on E-money, Obi Cubana, Senator Rochas Okorocha, Governor Hope Uzodimma. I am calling on all that matters in Igbo land. I may not remember all your names. All of you who have good human hearts. Anyidon, I am begging you to champion this. We need funds.

“Luckily today as I arrive at the Abuja airport, E-Money gave me 250,000 Naira and that is actually what sustained me. Not just the kidney problem, I am bleeding inside. I don’t want to die. I don’t want to lose my children, they are too tender. I don’t want to leave them behind. Save and help me in any way you can.”
