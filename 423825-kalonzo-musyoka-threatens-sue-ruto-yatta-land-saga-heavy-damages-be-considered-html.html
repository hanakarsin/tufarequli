Kalonzo Musyoka Threatens to Sue Ruto over Yatta Land Saga: &quot;Heavy Damages to Be Considered&quot;

Publish date: 0001-01-01
  • Deputy President William Ruto accused Wiper leader Kalonzo Musyoka of grabbing National Youth Service (NYS) land in Yatta and converting it into his own personal farm
  • On Wednesday, August 25, he presented himself at the DCI offices
  • The former vice president claimed he was cleared of any wrongdoing by detectives
  • Kalonzo said he would consider taking legal action against the DP

Wiper leader Kalonzo Musyoka has threatened to sue Deputy President William Ruto over the Yatta land saga.

In a statement issued on Wednesday, August 25, after presenting himself to the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI), the former vice president said even though sleuths found he was 'clean', he won't let the matter rest.

He said the DP had tarnished his name, and as such, he would consider taking legal action against him.

"The DCI has found zero evidence of wrongdoing on my part. We don’t intend to leave this matter here, because my rights as a Kenyan citizen were violated by this pronouncement.”

"My legal team is still exploring other options because, for instance, heavy damages are clearly to be considered including whatever other legal remedies we may want to pursue.”

According to Ruto, the 200-acre land where his rural home sits belonged to the National Youth Service but was irregularly acquired by the Wiper Party leader.

Kalonzo accuses Ruto of land grabbing

Kalonzo denied claims he irregularly acquired the land and instead launched a scathing attack on the DP, alleging he was the 'chief priest of corruption' who had an appetite for land.

He mentioned several pieces of land which he claimed were grabbed by Ruto across the country.

"The Agricultural Development Corporation (ADC) land in Laikipia was grabbed by William Ruto and must revert to the State. He also took a loan at AFC to irregularly obtain land belonging to a former vice president in Kilgoris. "The National Land Commission (NLC) made a finding that Weston Hotel sits on land belonging to the Kenya Civil Aviation Authority (KCAA). An attempt was even made to grab the land belonging to Lang’ata Road Primary School; poor kids were teargassed, and we stood firm with them.”

Kalonzo matches to DCI

Kalonzo on Wednesday matched to the DCI headquarters along Kiambu Road with a battery of lawyers.

Some of the top lawyers who accompanied the former vice president were James Orengo (Siaya), Dan Maanzo (Makueni MP) and Mutula Kilonzo Jnr (Makueni senator).

It was not the first time Kalonzo presented himself at DCI. In January, he appeared before detectives over the controversial land.
