Jubilee MP Maina Kamanda backs David Murathe's call for Ruto retirement

Publish date: 2024-04-12

- Jubilee nominated MP Maina Kamanda said party's vice-chairperson David Murathe was right to call for Deputy President William Ruto's retirement in 2022

- The former Starehe MP argued Murathe's sentiments represented voice of silent majority in Jubilee

- Kamanda said when 2022 General Election campaigns time comes, Jubilee MPs from Central Kenya would speak same voice with the party official

- He poured cold water on Ruto's 2022 presidential ambition saying uniting the country was priority, not politics

Jubilee nominated MP Maina Kamanda has defended party vice-chairman David Murathe's call on Deputy President William Ruto to retire in 2022 when President Uhuru Kenyatta's term ends.

The bold MP who was recently nominated to Vision 2030 board said Murathe's sentiments that there was no power succession deal between Uhuru and Ruto was the voice of the silent majority.

In a report by Nation, seen by TUKO.co.ke, on Friday, December 28, the MP said said the party official's statement was a true reflection of Mt Kenya region's position in the 2022 General Election.

"Murathe's views are representative of the majority silent and those condemning him should awaken and know he was speaking the truth," Kamanda said.

The former Starehe MP said the country's leadership was focused on attaining national unity but true positions similar to Murathe's revelation will come to the open when campaigns for the next poll begin.

"We will all be echoing Murathe's sentiments when the right time comes. He is saying the truth and when that time for elections come we shall say the same things. Those dismissing him should go slow because his words represent what a bigger percentage of Jubilee members are saying," Kamanda said.

His statement has added a new twist to the 2022 succession politics debate, him being the first Jubilee affiliated leader to back the position which was rubbished and hotly contested by his counterparts from the leading party.

Speaking in Vihiga county on Wednesday, December 26, Murathe cheered up the dominantly Opposition audience bey declaring there was no Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) between Uhuru and Ruto that guaranteed the latter's succession ticket.

The nominated legislator argued silencing the Jubilee vice-chairman was similar to sweeping litter under the carpet as it would soon come out.

"Murathe has just exposed the wound. If it were hidden for long, it could be discovered at a time where it was no longer treatable. The revelation by Murathe should be embraced without much fuss. For now we need to focus on uniting country not politics," he added.

He praised Opposition leader Raila Oding'as reconciliation with Uhuru through the famous handshake saying it was reason Kenyans were enjoying a peaceful Christmas festive season.
