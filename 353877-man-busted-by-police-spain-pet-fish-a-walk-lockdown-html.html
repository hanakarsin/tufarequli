Man busted by police in Spain for taking pet fish for walk during lockdown

Publish date: 2024-07-29

- Spain had announced a total lockdown but cancelled it for some hours to allow its citizens to walk their pets

- One man decided to take his pet fish in its bowl out for a walk and was nabbed while at it

- It emerged he was only using the opportunity to relieve himself from the lockdown

The coronavirus lockdown in various countries has put human resilience to the test and anyone will just do anything to walk out of their compounds.

A Spanish man showed how desperate he was to get the fresh air after he was arrested while taking his fsh for a 'walk'.

Spain enforced a lockdown and other safety measures to ensure its citizens were not infected by the dreaded coronavirus but on Friday, April 24, cancelled the lockdown for a few hours to give its citizens an allowance to step out and walk their pets.

Well, one fishy pet owner thought it wise to also use the opportunity to step out and enjoy fresh air and the sun using his fish as an excuse.

While other pet owners used the opportunity to walk their dogs and cats, the man took advantage of the moment to get himself some fresh air.

The slippery man whose name is still a mystery was found sitting on a bench with his scaled friend inside his bowl in Logroño, northern Spain.

The man was immediately arrested and held at a nearby police station.

It is not yet clear if he was released or not but his action cracked many people on social media who wondered what he was thinking.

TUKO.co.ke has learnt he is not the first person to pull such a move in the country amid a strict coronavirus lockdown.

In March, police in Murcia arrested a man wearing a T-rex costume wandering the streets.

It should be noted Spain is among the leading countries as far as coronavirus deaths are concerned with confirmed 23,000 fatalities for far.

The country, which is the second after the US in coronavirus cases, reported 226,629 positive cases as of Sunday, April 26.
