Zainab Chidzuga: Family Apologises to Politicians Who Were Denied Chance to Speak at Her Funeral

Publish date: 2024-07-07

  • Consequently, political leaders including Kwale Governor Salim Mvurya and Senator Issa Boy left

The family of former Kwale woman representative Zainab Chidzuga has apologised for the drama that occurred during her internment.

Chidzuga was on Friday, August 13, buried at her home in Matuga’s Ziwanni Golani area in Kwale County.

The 65-year-old politician was laid to rest in line with Muslim traditions in a ceremony that was attended by close family members, friends and politicians.

No politicking

Initially, the family's spokesperson Chirau Ali Mwakwere had insisted that no politician would be allowed to address the congregation.

On the contrary, Chidzuga’s daughter Mariam Chidzuga asked Mwakwere to allow Agriculture PS Hamadi Boga to speak, but he declined, insisting on adhering to the prior agreement.

"If you are here to speak politics you can leave. If you are mourning with the family, you can stay," said Mwakwere

Consequently, political leaders including Kwale Governor Salim Mvurya, Senator Issa Boy, Lungalunga MP Khatib Mwashetani and Kinango MP Benjamin Tayari left.

Further, some mourners, who also protested the decision left.

Following the incident, the family has since apologised to the Kwale people and politicians who had assembled to help them inter their kin.

Illness

Chidzuga, who died on Thursday, August 12, had been ailing for some time and died after hospitalisation at Reliance Hospital in South C Nairobi.

She is believed to have died from coronavirus-related complications.

The politician was last seen in public during the burial of former ODM activist Hassan Chitembe in Tiribe in Matuga in Kwale on July 17.

The former official of Maendeleo Ya Wanawake Organization (MYWO), was elected Kwale women representative on an ODM ticket in 2013.

Towards the 2017 general election, she defected to the Jubilee Party and lost the election to ODM’s Zulekha Hassan.
