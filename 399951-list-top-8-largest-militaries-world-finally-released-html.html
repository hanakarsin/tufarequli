China tops list of top 8 largest militaries in the world

Publish date: 2024-04-02

- The latest 2021 military ranking by Global Firepower has rated China as having the world largest military

- The Asian giant reportedly has about 2.19 million active military personnel at its disposal

- The country on the second spot is India, with 1.45 million active military personnel

- India is followed closely by the US with 1.4 million military personnel

Global powers have finally been ranked by Global Fire Power using potential military strength across the globe.

In the first position was China which is said to have 2, 185, 000 active military personnel followed by India which has 1, 445, 000 active military personnel.

The Asian countries were followed by the United States of America (USA) in third place with 1.4 million active military personnel while North Korea followed in the fourth position with 1.3 million.

In fifth place was Russia with 1,014,000 active military personnel followed by Pakistan in sixth place with 654,000 active military personnel.

"In terms of manpower, no military in the world comes close to China. However, when it comes to the overall power of the world's military forces, the US comes out on top, followed by Russia and China," Statista tweeted.

South Korea came in position seven with 600,000 active military personnel while Iran took the eight spot with 525,000 active military personnel.

No African country was found on the top eight list.

The finalized Global Firepower ranking utilised over 50 individual factors to determine a given nation's PowerIndex score with categories ranging from military might and financials to logistical capability and geography.

While a rating of 0.000 would represent the perfect score, it is considered realistically unattainable.

The parade marked the end of a political congress at which leader Kim Jong Un admitted severe failings but pledged to boost the country’s military might.

Clad in a black leather coat, gloves and fur hat, Kim smiled and waved as he watched the parade in a flood-lit Kim Il Sung Square.

Photos released by state media showed the SLBM was labelled Pukguksong-5, potentially marking an upgrade over the Pukguksong-4 that was unveiled at a larger military parade in October.

“The world’s most powerful weapon, submarine-launch ballistic missiles, entered the square one after another, powerfully demonstrating the might of the revolutionary armed forces,” state news agency KCNA reported.
