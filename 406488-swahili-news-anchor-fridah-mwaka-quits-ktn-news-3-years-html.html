Swahili News Anchor Fridah Mwaka Quits KTN News After 3 Years

Publish date: 2024-05-14

- Fridah Mwaka aired her final news broadcast on Sunday during which she bid farewell to her fans

- The TV girl thanked everyone including her audience and colleagues who made her stay worthwhile

- The journalist joined KTN in 2018 and is a former presenter on Kubamba Radio and ex-contestant on The Presenter TV show

KTN's news desk has bid goodbye to another news anchor in the Swahili department after Fridah Mwaka's exit.

The TV girl took part in her last broadcast at the Standard Group owned station on Sunday, March 21, where she also bid farewell to her fans.

Mwaka thanked the viewers for cheering her on and making her even better in her three years stint at KTN.

She noted it was time to depart asking that they let her leave in one peace and thanked everyone including her former employers and colleagues.

The news presenter hoped she and her fans would meet on a different platform and continue to interact.

In a post on social media, she wrote:

"It has been my pride to walk with you on the journey of fun events. But more are the marks that have stuck in my heart for supporting me and making me better in the media industry. The time has come to leave KTN news Kenya. Allow me to say goodbye."

Mwaka joined KTN back in August 2018 and leaves as one of the most revered Swahili news anchors in Kenya.

She was also a contestant on The Presenter talent show which aired on KTN way before her appointment.

Before joining KTN, she was a presenter on Kubamba Radio.

KTN losing presenters

Mwaka's exit comes just a couple of months after KTN lost another news presenter in Grace Kuria to a rival.

She made the announcement while signing out of the weekend bulletin this Sunday, January 1.

"I would also like to take this opportunity to say thank you to God, thank you to my family, thank you to Standard Group management, thank you to my colleagues and thank you to you the live viewer for all the love, for the criticism, and for the words of encouragement," she said.

Apart from being a weekend news anchor at KTN, she hosted another show called “Beyond the Scars” every Sunday morning. As at the time of her exit, she had served at KTN for two years.

Her passion for telling human-interest stories served as the stepping stone that saw her move from behind the camera to the one on the shot.
