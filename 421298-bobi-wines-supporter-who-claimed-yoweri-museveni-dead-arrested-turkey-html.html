Bobi Wine's Supporter Who Claimed Yoweri Museveni was Dead Arrested in Turkey

Uganda's opposition party, the National Unity Platform (NUP), has protested the arrest of popular Facebook and YouTube vlogger Fred Lumbuye, a supporter of the political outfit.

Lumbuye was on Tuesday, August 3, arrested by security agents in Instanbul, Turkey, after he was suspected of being the mastermind behind the rumours that President Yoweri Museveni had died after contracting the dreaded COVID-19.

Lumbuye also claimed the Kabaka was dead

The firebrand blogger, a political exile in Turkey, had also previously announced that the Kabaka of Buganda, Ronald Muwenda Mutebi, was dead.

Taking to Twitter on Wednesday, August 4, NUP, through its party leader Robert Kyagulanyi Ssentamu alias Bobi Wine accused Turkey of failing to protect the rights of political dissidents and exiles who have sought refuge in the country.

"The governments of the world ought to protect the rights of political dissidents and exiles who have sought refuge in their countries." Likewise, Ugandans abroad ought to feel safe,'' said Bobi Wine.

Bobi Wine observed that Lumbuye's remarks were personal

Bobi Wine further observed that Lumbuye's remarks don't reflect the party's position, terming them his own opinions.

''Like we have been advocating for the rights of all Ugandans who have been subjected to the regime injustices, the same applies to Lumbuye. His opinions don't represent NUP's official position. Everyone is entitled to their own opinions. We come in whenever the need arises to give guidance,'' Bobi Wine added.

The rumours about Museveni's death continued even when the 76-year-old head of state who has been in power for over 35 years appeared in public at Munyonyo and Bombo in the company Deputy President William Ruto to launch a biochemical factory.

Lumbuye's arrest comes barely a month after Museveni scoffed at social media reports that pronounce him dead.

Museveni, who became president of Uganda in 1986, directed security agencies to arrest a racket of bloggers who announced he was dead.

''The security service also needs to solve this problem. I need to check with them to locate quickly the one who started that story because they waste people's time,'' said Museveni.

Museveni directed security officials to crack the whip on his distractors

The president said his security forces would locate and go for all the individuals behind the rumours about his death.

''And if you are in Europe, we are going to denounce you so that you go to hell,'' said the head of state.

He spoke during another swearing-in ceremony of cabinet ministers at Kololo Independence grounds on Thursday, July 8, maintaining that security should solve what he described as a non-security but rather an idiotic problem.

The rumours started circulating on the night of June 26 in the form of multimedia from anonymous sources on microblogging platforms, including the government-banned Facebook mobile application.
