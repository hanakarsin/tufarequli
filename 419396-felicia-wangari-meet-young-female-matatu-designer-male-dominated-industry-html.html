Felicia Wangari: Meet Young Female Matatu Graffiti Designer Taking on Male-Dominated Industry

Publish date: 2024-05-11
  • An ambitious young lady has set her sights on making more women matatu graphic designers
  • Felicia Wangari's father was a matatu graffiti designer, and she fell in love with the trade as a child
  • After his passing, she resolved to continue his legacy and incorporate more women into the trade

Colourful matatus (public transport vehicles) are arguably one of Kenya's most significant cultural exports.

Their unique designs have captured the attention of international celebrities, including musicians Chris Brown, Cardi B, actor Robert “T-Bag” Knepper, among others.

However, the designers behind the eye-catching rides are primarily men, a trend a young lady aspires to change.

Speaking to TUKO.co.ke, Felicia Wangari, alias Kafely, said she wants to inspire more women to join the trade.

According to the young lady, her late father was a matatu designer, and she wants to continue his legacy.

“My late father was a matatu designer, and he would bring his equipment home. As a result, I developed an interest in the craft, and when he passed away, I resolved to continue his legacy,” she said.

Kafely offered that throughout her school years, she would draw in class and is currently finessing her skills at Lithiumized Artschool, which was set up by one of Kenya’s most prominent matatu designers, Mose.

Kafely further said that her male counterparts welcomed her to the industry with open arms.

“I got a warm welcome, and we are working well alongside each other,” she said.

Kafely’s dream

Kafely is set to complete her training in a month, and she looks forward to accomplishing her goals.

“I dream of starting my matatu graffiti designing outfit and get more women to join this industry. It should not be a men-only affair,” she said.

To the youths decrying the high unemployment rate, she said:

“Do not depend on looking for a job. Instead, create employment.”

Kafely also lauded her teacher Mose for always encouraging her to pursue her dream.

“He always tells me that I can make it,” she said.

While admitting that the industry is male-dominated, Mose called upon industry players to support Kafely.

"The culture has always been about male designers. I think it is time for gender equality. Let us support the girl child, soon she will become a done in this industry,” he stated.

In June, a young South African lady inspired other women after sharing a post about being a female in the mining industry.

She urged other women not to fear joining the male-dominated industry.

“Let’s disrupt what is known to be a male-dominated industry,” she captioned a photo wearing blue overalls.
