A Kisumu man whose body has been lying in the mortuary for over four years is set to be finally buried after years of wrangles about his final resting place.

Barrack Ogada who served as a tea picker in Tinderet, Nandi county, lost his life in 2014.

After his death, his family was stopped by one James Onunga who laid claim to their piece of land in Tieng'rea, Kisumu county.

According to the Daily Nation, Ogada's family said Onunga accompanied by some police officers returned their kin's body to the morgue leaving them in agony.

Ogada's burial and exhumation

In 2017, the family managed to successfully bury their beloved one after obtaining a court order but Onunga a month later also obtained another court order ordering the exhumation of Ogada's remains.

Onunga once again in the company of the men in uniform and some youths exhumed the body in a dramatic incident that saw Ogada's family lobbed with teargas.

During the fracas, Ogada's house which was constructed before his death according to Luo traditions was destroyed. The roof which was removed from the top of his house remained at the scene to date.

The case moved to court where Onunga sued Ogada's family, The Standard reported. He claimed he bought the piece of the disputed land from Ogada's grandfather, Francis Agoo, in 1986, and got its title in 1990.

Onunga loses case

The plaintiff, however, lost his case to the defendants twice but appealed the ruling.

On Wednesday, June 9, Onunga for the third time lost his case after he failed to provide sufficient evidence that he owns the land.

Senior Resident Magistrate Winfred Onkunya ordered the land registrar in Kisumu to cancel Onunga's titled deed and replace his name with that of Francis Agoo.

Ogada's family was overjoyed with the ruling and plan to put the emotional torture they have suffered by finally burying their kin.

His mortuary bills have since accumulated to KSh 800,000 and they want Onunga to foot them after the case was dismissed with costs.

They also appealed to well-wishers to come to their rescue.

Machakos woman buried after 17 years

In a related story reported by TUKO.co.ke, a Machakos woman was laid to rest after lying in mortuary for 17 years.

Esther Nzakwa Kitovo died on August 31, 2004, and her remains had been lying at the Machakos Funeral Home.

A protracted land dispute delayed her burial as one of her step-sons filed a court case insisting he was the owner of the home where she was to be buried in 2004.

On Saturday, April 24, she was laid to rest in her son's home pending determination of the land case.
