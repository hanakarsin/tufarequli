Salim Sagar: Missing Mombasa Businessman Reunites With Family after a Month in Abduction

Publish date: 2024-06-03
  • Salim Sagar, 40, was found on the morning of Sunday, September 19, and he is currently with his family
  • Sagar's abductors allegedly warned him not to talk to human rights actors or even journalists concerning his disappearance
  • The businessman was abducted on August 18, 2021, by people suspected to be police officers

A missing Mombasa-based businessman facing terror charges has finally reunited with his family after a month in the hands of abductors.

Abdulhakim Salim Sagar, 40, was fighting terrorism-related charges at a court in Shanzu, Mombasa, was found on the morning of Sunday, September 19, and he is now with his family.

Not to say a thing to journalists

According to HAKI Africa, the abductors warned him not to say anything concerning his abduction to human rights actors or journalists.

"One is tortured, the constitution and law are flagrantly violated. One day we will have justice, but for now...Aluta continua!" the human rights activists stated.

Sagar's abduction

Sagar was allegedly abducted on Wednesday, August 18, 2021, by people suspected to be police officers.

On that day, seven armed men accosted Sagar and bundled him into a waiting Toyota Hilux double-cabin pickup car before speeding off.

He had just attended prayers at a Mosque in Kuze, Old Town, before the incident.

Five days later, coast regional security committee chairman John Elungatta revealed that police officers had arrested the terror suspect.

According to Mbugua Mureithi, the family’s lawyer, Sagar, who runs a cybercafe business in Old Town, Mombasa, was charged with possessing terror-related materials on his mobile phone.

''Since the case started in 2018, he has never skipped any case. I am shocked by the way they arrested my brother in front of his wife and children.

We demand those who have taken him to bring him before the court. He has been cooperating with the officers. Even when he was asked to appear at the ATPU offices in Mombasa, he obliged,” Mureithi said.

Sagar was first taken into custody in 2018, following the arrest of his employee.
