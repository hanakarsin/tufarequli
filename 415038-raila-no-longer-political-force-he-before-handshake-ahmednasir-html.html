Raila is No Longer the Political Force He was before Handshake, Lawyer Ahmednasir

Publish date: 2024-05-11

- Ahmednasir Abdullahi opined that the Opposition leader's 2018 truce with President Uhuru Kenyatta watered down his political vigour

- The city lawyer made the remarks while qualifying why Raila should not team up with Deputy President William Ruto

- He said the 2022 presidential election would only be energetic and interesting if Raila runs against Ruto

Constitutional lawyer Ahmednasir Abdullahi has said ODM party leader Raila Odinga is no longer the political force he was before the handshake.

The lawyer opined that the Opposition leader's 2018 truce with President Uhuru Kenyatta watered down his political influence, reducing him to a mere errand boy.

Ahmednasir alias Grand Mullah made the remarks on Citizen TV on Wednesday, June 9, while trying to justify why Raila should not team up with Deputy President William Ruto ahead of 2022.

“What will Raila bring to the table in a union with Ruto? He brings nothing. Raila is not the potent political force he was prior to the handshake. The handshake damaged him a lot; he became a poodle and a stooge," said Ahmednasir.

The senior counsel added that ODM supporters were wishing Raila teams up with the second in command to be politically relevant in the forthcoming General Election.

However, the lawyer said the 2022 presidential election would only be energetic and interesting if Raila runs against Ruto.

In a previous report by TUKO.co.ke, the city lawyer claimed the Ruto-Raila alliance would be "disastrous" and might not yield the desired outcome.

He argued Raila had lost the ground and would add little value.

"It will be a total disaster for Ruto if Raila joins him. He will cause mayhem in his coalition," tweeted.

The lawyer argued the ODM leader's association with the president tainted his reputation and finished him politically.

Weighing on the proposed alignment, political commentator and Law scholar Makau Mutua said the Ruto-Raila alliance would most likely lose to the One Kenya Alliance.

One Kenya Alliance brought together Kalonzo Musyoka (Wiper), Musalia Mudavadi (ANC), Moses Wetang'ula (Ford Kenya) and Gideon Moi (KANU).

The team reportedly had the blessings of President Uhuru Kenya.
