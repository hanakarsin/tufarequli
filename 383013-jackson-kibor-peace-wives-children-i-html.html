Controversial Eldoret-based tycoon Jackson Kibor has finally made peace with his wives and children after years of battles over his KSh 6 billion wealth.

The 86-year-old veteran politician who is also a farmer in Uasin Gishu has made headlines in recent years over court cases, polygamy and boychild issues.

Speaking to The Star, the straight shooting octogenarian described his past as "horrible" but clarified he did not regret his background life because it made him who he is today.

“My background was horrible but that is in the past and I have no regrets because that life made me who I am today," Kibor said.

Ready to die

Despite his old age, the businessman still stands tall and is energetic, attributing his good health to lots of physical exercise and drinking just one beer in a week.

The cantankerous old man, however, said he was ready to die having seen it all in his life.

He urged his family members to be patient so they can access his wealth, vowing to distribute it before his death through his own goodwill.

“I will only give them wealth as I wish," he said.

Court battles

In early 2020, Mzee Kibor moved to court in a vicious legal battle with his six children who wanted him to allocate them a share of his land.

The octogenarian told the court he was not sure the children were his and that he had not married their mothers.

The court allowed the retired politician to subject the kids to a DNA test but he has now changed his mind and says he cannot be buried with the wealth, prompting him to forgive all his family members.

“Those who have mistreated and disrespected me will get less while those who recognise me as their father and owner of the property have to get more. Some will get as little as two acres while others will get as many as 200 acres," he argued.

Kibor's six children are Ezekiel Kipngetich, Elkana Kipleting, Evans Kipkosgei, Eric Kipchumba, Raymond Kibitok and Edwin Kipkoech.

The farmer married his fourth wife in 2018 after winning a divorce case against his third wife of 52 years.
